About Steve Jobs

Steve Jobs was an American entrepreneur, inventor, and industrial designer. He was the co-founder, chairman, and CEO of Apple Inc., and played a key role in the development of revolutionary products such as the Macintosh, iPod, iPhone, and iPad. Jobs was also a major force behind Pixar Animation Studios and served on the board of The Walt Disney Company after Pixar's acquisition.

Major Achievements

1976 - Co-founded Apple Inc.

Revolutionized personal computing with the Apple I and Apple II computers.

1984 - Launched the Macintosh

Introduced the first mass-market personal computer with a graphical user interface.

1997 - Returned to Apple

Led Apple’s turnaround with innovative products and design.

2001-2010 - iPod, iPhone, iPad

Transformed the music, phone, and tablet industries with groundbreaking devices.

Pixar & Disney

Acquired Pixar and helped create some of the most successful animated films in history.
